# YancoCup — Vision

## What YancoCup is

YancoCup is a **soccer prediction platform** where friends compete to prove who knows the game best. It wraps live scores, news, and match data around a prediction engine that makes every fixture feel personal.

The product wedge is simple: **predictions with friends, not scores alone.**

FotMob owns polished stats. Flashscore owns speed. SofaScore owns data depth. OneFootball owns news. None of them own the social prediction experience for friend groups. That's the gap YancoCup fills.

## The core loop

Everything in YancoCup serves this cycle:

```
See upcoming matches
    → Predict (fast, satisfying, 2 taps on mobile)
        → See what friends predicted (tension before kickoff)
            → Match happens (live score updates keep you informed)
                → Points reveal (drama, animation, who nailed it)
                    → Leaderboard shift (who's climbing, who's falling)
                        → Pool chat (trash talk, reactions, bragging)
                            → Share moment (prediction card → WhatsApp/social)
                                → Come back tomorrow (streak, next deadline)
```

Every feature must serve this loop. If a proposed feature doesn't make the loop more engaging, more social, or more addictive — it gets cut or deprioritized.

## The feeling

When someone opens YancoCup, they should feel:

1. **"This knows my team."** Personalized: followed teams, next matches, pending predictions.
2. **"This is alive."** Live indicators, countdowns, real-time pool activity.
3. **"This is sharp."** Clean typography, fast loads, no jank, intentional motion.
4. **"My friends are here."** Pool standings visible, chat active, shared predictions.
5. **"I need to predict before kickoff."** Urgency: deadlines, streaks at risk, pool pressure.

It should NOT feel like:
- A data dump (too many stats nobody asked for)
- A hobby project (inconsistent spacing, broken states, placeholder content)
- A betting site (no real money, no odds-first language)
- A news aggregator (news supports the match experience, it's not the product)

## Product identity

**Name:** YancoCup
**Tagline:** "Predict. Compete. Prove it."
**Tone:** Confident, clean, slightly competitive. Not corporate, not childish.
**Language:** Soccer-native. "Match" not "game." "Fixture" not "event." "Kit" not "uniform."
**Personality:** The friend who always has the lineup ready, knows the form table by heart, and will remind you that you predicted a 3-0 and it ended 0-0.

## Audience

Primary: Soccer fans aged 18-35 who:
- Follow at least one league or competition seriously
- Have a friend group that watches matches together (physically or online)
- Enjoy predictions, fantasy, or competitive social games
- Use WhatsApp/Telegram as their main messaging platform
- May speak Arabic, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, or English

Secondary: Casual fans during the World Cup who want a fun way to engage with the tournament in a group setting.

## What YancoCup is NOT

| We are NOT | Because |
|------------|---------|
| A live score app | football-data.org free tier can't compete with FotMob/Flashscore speed |
| A stats platform | No xG, heatmaps, or player ratings in our data sources |
| A news site | AI-rewritten RSS is a supplement, not a destination |
| A betting platform | No real money. Never. This is bragging rights only. |
| A fantasy league | No player drafts, no transfers, no salary caps |
| A social network | Pools are focused groups, not public feeds |
| A 3D showcase | The globe is a signature element, not the product |

Six languages, RTL-aware:
- English (default)
- Arabic (RTL — critical for Middle East/North Africa audience)
- Spanish
- French
- German
- Portuguese

Arabic is not an afterthought. It's a competitive advantage — most soccer apps treat Arabic as a low-priority translation. YancoCup should feel native in Arabic.

## Unique differentiators

1. **Prediction pools with friends** — private groups, custom scoring, pool chat, matchday recaps, shareable results. Nobody does this well as a free web app.
2. **World Cup 2026 focus** — purpose-built for the biggest tournament, with bracket predictions, group stage tracking, and knockout multipliers.
3. **Arabic-first multilingual** — genuine RTL support, Arabic soccer terminology, not Google Translate quality.
4. **Zero cost to operate** — entirely free tier stack means this can run indefinitely without revenue pressure.
5. **Matchday ritual design** — the app has phases (pre-match urgency → live pulse → post-match reveal) that create habit loops.

## North star metrics

If YancoCup is working, these numbers go up:
- **Predictions per user per matchday** — are people predicting multiple matches?
- **Pool size** — are users inviting friends?
- **Return rate on matchday** — do users come back to see results?
- **Share rate** — are prediction cards / recap cards being sent to WhatsApp?
- **Streak length** — are users maintaining daily prediction streaks?
